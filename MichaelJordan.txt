Widely regarded as the greatest basketball player of all time, Michael Jordan's legacy transcends the sport itself. 
Drafted by the Chicago Bulls in 1984, Jordan's relentless work ethic, competitive drive, and scoring prowess defined an era. 
His six NBA championships, five MVP awards, and countless iconic moments — including the "Flu Game" and 
his game-winning shot in the 1998 Finals — solidified his place in basketball history. 
Jordan's influence extended off the court, turning him into a global icon through his partnership with Nike and the creation of the Air Jordan brand. 
His unmatched will to win and "killer mentality" became the gold standard for athletes worldwide.
